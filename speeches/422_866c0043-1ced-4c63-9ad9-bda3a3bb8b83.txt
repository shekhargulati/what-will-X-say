Your visit reflects the depth of our ties that have endured and enhanced over two centuries. And, our bonds are not limited to Governments.They extend to our people and societies, who shared our root in the pride. Our time has been time and distance .Today, they are a rich tapestry of friendship in diverse areas.\nFriends,\nMy discussions with Prime Minister Jugnauth has been warm and productive. Our conversation reminded me of my mission in Mauritius in March 2015. That visit, which was my first to the Indian Ocean region, gave us a strong agenda for cooperation. It also underscored the commonality of our values, interests and efforts.\n\nFriends,\nToday, we have taken another leap in our bilateral agenda. As frontline states of the Indian Ocean, Prime Minister Jugnauth and I agree that it is our responsibility to ensure collective maritime security around our coasts and in our EEZs. We agree that effective management of conventional and non-conventional threats in the Indian Ocean is to pursue economic opportunities; protect the livelihood of our communities, and provide security to our people And, for this India-Mauritius is very very important\nWe have to keep up our vigil against:\nPiracy that impacts trade and tourism;\nTrafficking of drugs and humans;\nIllegal fishing, and\nOther forms of illegal exploitation of marine resources\n\nThe conclusion of the bilateral Maritime Security Agreement will strengthen our mutual cooperation and capacities. We have also agreed to strengthen our wide-ranging cooperation in hydrography for a safe and peaceful marine domain. India is supporting the National Coast Guard of Mauritius in augmenting its capacity through Project Trident.We have also taken a novel, the grant of the Coast Guard Ship Guardian, a grant assistance program under Mauritius to provide.\nFriends,\nA strong developmental partnership with Mauritius is a hallmark of our engagement. India is proud to participate in the ongoing development activities in Mauritius.The agreement today on a 500 million US dollar Line of Credit from India to Mauritius is a good example of our strong and continuing commitment toward the development of Mauritius. It will also help in the implementation of priority projects.Prime Minister Jugnauth and I welcome the progress in ongoing projects. India will extend full support for the timely execution of projects identified among our countries.Projects that will vitalize the economy of Mauritius and our relations in a qualitative transformation. Our discussions in, we also see Mauritius with skill development cooperation. It is a active part of our current interaction under India's multiple capacity building program with Mauritius. We are happy to open in this domain.\nFriends,\nSigning and ratification of the framework agreement on International Solar Alliance by Mauritius has opened up new territories of regional partnership for both countries in this field.\nFriends,\nWe take pride in this contribution of the Indian-origin community to the national life of Mauritius. To promote our prosperous links with the diaspora in Mauritius, India had, in January this year, declared a special carve-out on OCI cards only for Mauritius. Our flag carrier airlines have agreed to increase their code sharing arrangement to new destinations. This too is expected to lead to greater tourism and people-to-people\nFriends,\nApart from bilateral issues, Prime Minister Jugnauth and I also have a number of regional and global issues. We have agreed to continue supporting each other in multilateral fora and our common challenges and interests on close cooperation. Prime Minister Jugnauth's visit elevating to the contribution our relationship to new heights I thank Prime Minister Jugnauth for his vision and support to our relationship. And I look forward to working in the coming months as well.
