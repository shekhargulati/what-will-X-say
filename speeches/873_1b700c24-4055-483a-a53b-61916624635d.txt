"\nHis Excellency Prime Minister Mr. Mark Rutte, and Members of the Media,\nI am delighted to welcome Prime Minister Rutte and his eminent delegation to India I am particularly pleased that Prime Minister, Rutte is leading a large trade delegation of over 80 Dutch companies.\nPrime Minister Rutte brings rich business experience to his career\nHis decision to brave the Delhi heat to visit.\nAlthough a relatively small country, Netherlands has a long history of remarkable achievements and global leadership in many areas.\nOur two countries are bound by history and shared values We have similar concerns like terrorism and common aspirations for a safe and stable world. The Netherlands hosts the second largest community in Europe.\nSeveral Dutch companies have a long presence in India They are likely to be seen as Indian as Dutch Today, the Netherlands is the fifth largest source of investments in India.\nIndia is the fifth largest source of investment in the Netherlands. It is a reflection of its competitive and open business environment. The Netherlands has emerged as an important platform for Indian companies in the European Union, which is one of our biggest markets.\nSo, I am happy that Prime Minister Rutte is visiting us. We had a comprehensive and productive dialogue today. We discussed bilateral relations as well as global and regional issues of mutual interest. We were later joined by 35 business leaders.\nWe agreed that we are going to benefit from closer bilateral and multilateral collaboration, including areas like countering terrorism and extremism, defense, maritime security and cyber security\nWe have agreed to set up a Joint Working Group on Counter Terrorism, which will hold its first meeting on 19th June.\nI thanked Prime Minister Rutte for Dutch support for India's permanent membership of a reformed United Nations Security Council. I also thanked him for his support for India's candidature for the Security Council in 2021-22.\nOur agreements today are closely aligned with India's economic priorities.\nPrime Minister Rutte expressed support for our Mission in India, including in the defense sector.\nThe agreement on manufacturing of dredgers in India at the Cochin Shipyard is a welcome step in our efforts to develop Indian shipyards.\nSimilarly, the agreement on manufacturing of Measles, and Rubella Vaccine in India, with transfer of technology, represents yet another step to develop India's manufacturing sector, and improve healthcare.\nWe have also agreed to leverage Dutch expertise in developing coastal roads and metro lines in India\nWe have agreed to set up a joint work group on Skill Development.\nIn the renewable energy sector, I am happy that we are participating in the development of an electric vehicle ecosystem in India.\nWe are setting up centers of excellence in areas like horticulture and animal husbandry. These are areas of strong dutch competence\nWe will soon host a Dutch team for a road-map for the collaboration in the National Clean Ganga Mission.\nMore broadly, the joint statement outlines our road-map for the priority sectors of mutual interest, including infrastructure development, shipping, ports and inland waterways; renewable energy; science \u0026 technology; health and sports\nOur discussion with business leaders was helpful in understanding their future. I was highly encouraged by the deep interest and enthusiasm on both sides to form deeper partnerships.\nI have also been invited to visit the Netherlands in India. This will boost Dutch tourism in India and increase our people-to-people ties.\nOur productive interactions today is reaffirming my view of the Netherlands as a key partner in India's economic development.\nYour visit has set the stage to take our ties to the next level and realizes the full potential of our relationship.\nThank you."
